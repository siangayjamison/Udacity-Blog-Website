Sincerely, Your Future Black Girl in Tech

Truth or dare is a game I’ve played all my life. But I never knew a kiddy game would have such a huge impact on it. But here we are! I started playing truth or dare by myself and I’ve never felt better. Truth is, being daring is what’s pushing my foot through the tech industry door.

My name is Siangay Jamison. I am a 21-year-old college dropout looking to become a FrontEnd Web Developer or product manager. I am a multi-hyphenate creative with 3+ years of experience in public relations, social media, and graphic design. My first dare was my decision to leave college (in my senior year). I know, *cue the confused meme*. I have a supportive family, but I know they thought I was crazy. I can’t blame them. I was a public relations major and had been working with a public relations agency for 2 years. I was pretty set up for after graduation.

Truth was, I wasn’t happy. I wasn’t fulfilled with the work I was doing. I couldn’t take it anymore and dared myself to be happy. So what next? I didn’t know. All I knew was that I had to figure something out & fast. I came across a “day in the life of a product designer” video on TikTok and decided to look back into UX/UI design. I used to be interested in it, but dropped the idea of it because my college didn’t offer it as a major. This is what started my journey in trying to crack the code to break into tech.

I spent hours on #techtok & #techtwitter every day absorbing as much information that I could. Within the next week, I decided that I wanted to become a frontend developer, optimized my LinkedIn page, updated my new ATS safe resume, and had coffee chats scheduled.

That was in November ’21. Now in May ’22, I have tech newbies asking me for advice, went viral on LinkedIn, became a Year Up student, and recipient of Udacity x OneTen Blacks in Technology scholarship for FrontEnd Web Development.

I’m not there yet, but I am definitely on the brink of it.

Long story short, I dared myself to do what I wanted. Truth is, you can do it too!

Sincerely, Your future black girl in tech.
